3.3 Special matrices

In the special case where a matrix has the same numbers of rows and columns, it is said to be square. If A⊤=A\mathbf{A}^{\scriptstyle\top}=\mathbf{A}A⊤=A, the matrix is said to be symmetric.

Symmetric:[122−1]Not symmetric:[320−1]\text{Symmetric:} \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 1 & 2 \\ 2 & -1 \end{array} \right] \qquad \text{Not symmetric:} \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 3 & 2 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array} \right]Symmetric:[12​2−1​]Not symmetric:[30​2−1​]
